Why does Warm Dusk need a one-time pairing?

TVs don't let apps draw over other apps unless the owner grants it — that's a good rule. The setup uses the same official pairing mechanism developers use to grant those permissions, once, and touches nothing else. Everything it enables is revocable anytime in your TV's settings.

Questions

Fair questions, straight answers.

Does it work over Netflix and YouTube?

Yes — everything. Warm Dusk draws over every app, every input and the home screen. That's the whole point.

Will it survive turning the TV off?

Yes. Warm Dusk starts with the TV and remembers your schedule. Set it once.

Does it collect anything?

No. No analytics, no ads, no accounts — and no internet permission at all, so the app physically cannot send anything, ever. Don't take our word for it: every release publishes its SHA-256, and the APK's manifest is inspectable with standard Android tools. It will stay this way, because it's the reason many of you are here.

What if my remote has no mute button?

Teach Warm Dusk any button: settings → "Panel button" → hold the key you want. Home-row shortcuts work regardless.

Is the setup safe?

It uses the same official pairing mechanism Android provides to developers, and it grants exactly two things: "draw over other apps" (the filter itself) and an accessibility service (so the app can notice you holding the mute key — key events only, and with no internet permission nothing can leave the TV regardless). Both are visible and revocable in your TV's settings anytime.

Which TVs work?

Any Android TV / Google TV device on Android 8 or newer, plus Fire TV. See the compatibility table for tested models.
